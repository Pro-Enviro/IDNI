Infrared Heating

This wavelength of the electromagnetic spectrum provides a large but gentle spread of heat known as ‘comfort heat’ and works like the Sun’s non-visible part of the spectrum. Far-infrared heaters require less power to create heat and typically run at 98.5% efficiency, with a quicker heat-up period compared to near-infrared heaters.

Stratified Warm Air

The production areas contain high apex ceilings. Such areas suffer from stratification of warm air that rises to the apex of the ceiling, which then is eventually lost to the atmosphere. This phenomenon increases the load factor of the associated heating system as it tackles the heat loss from the operative level.

toys_fan

De-Stratification Fans

Warm air rises in buildings with high ceilings, and heat is lost through the roof. De-stratification fans blow warm air back to ground level where it is needed and can save up to 20% on typical energy bills.

Heating Controls

The heating system is controlled via temperature and time controls.Additionally, the radiators have TRVs (Thermostatic Radiator Valves) which increase the resolution of control. Due to this efficient level of control, the system only operates when required, reducing wasted heat generation.
